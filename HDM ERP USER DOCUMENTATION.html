1.2 Accessing HDM ERP

HDM ERP is available on multiple platforms:

  • Web App: https://hdmerp.pxxl.click
  • Admin Panel: Accessible by super admins for system configuration
  • Desktop App: Download from the landing page (Windows/Mac/Linux)
  • Mobile App: Available on Google Play Store & Apple App Store

1.3 Registration & Activation

1
Register Your Company

Click "Get Started" on the landing page. Choose a plan (Free Trial, Standard, Pro, or Enterprise). Fill in your company name, your full name, email, phone number, and password.

2
Complete Payment (Paid Plans)

Choose your payment method: M-Pesa (STK Push, Send Money, Paybill, or Till) or Stripe (credit/debit card). Follow the instructions and submit.

3
Get Your License Key

For Free Trial: you receive a license key immediately. For paid plans: the admin approves your payment and you receive the key via email.

4
Activate Your Device

Open the activation page. Enter your license key. Your device is now trusted and you can log in.

Note: Keep your license key safe. You'll need it if you switch devices or clear your browser data.

1.4 Logging In

Enter your registered email and password. Check "Remember me" to stay logged in on this device. If you're on a new device, you'll need to activate it first with your license key.

1.5 Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is your home screen. It shows:

  • Total Customers — Number of customers in your database
  • Open Invoices — Unpaid invoices and their total
  • Revenue — Total income from paid invoices and payments
  • Low Stock Items — Products below reorder level
  • Cash Flow — Income vs Expenses summary
  • Recent Orders — Latest 5 sales orders

Use the sidebar to navigate between modules. Only modules enabled for your plan are visible.

2. Finance Module

The Finance module is the core of your accounting system. It follows double-entry bookkeeping — every transaction must balance (debits = credits).

2.1 Chart of Accounts

Accounts are categories for your financial transactions. Types include: Asset, Liability, Equity, Income, Expense, and Cost of Sales.

1
Add an Account: Go to Finance → Accounts. Click "+ Add Account". Enter a unique Code, Name, Type, Description, and Opening Balance. Click Create.

The account balance updates automatically when journal entries are posted.

2.2 Journal Entries

Journal entries record double-entry transactions. Every financial event (revenue, expense, invoice payment, bill payment, payroll) creates a journal entry.

1
Add Manual Entry: Go to Finance → Journal. Click "+ New Entry". Enter Date, Description, and optional Reference. Add at least 2 lines — select an account, enter Debit or Credit amount. Total Debits must equal Total Credits. Click "Post Entry".
Important: Debits must always equal Credits. The system will not allow unbalanced entries.

2.3 Invoices

Invoices bill your customers for goods or services. Status flow: Draft → Sent → Paid (or Cancelled).

1
Create Invoice: Go to Finance → Invoices. Click "+ Create Invoice". Search/select or type a Customer name. Set Invoice Date and Due Date. Add line items (description, quantity, unit price, tax rate). Click Create.

Actions: Send (changes to Sent), Mark Paid (auto-creates journal entry: Debit Cash, Credit Revenue), Cancel, Print.

2.4 Bills

Bills record what you owe to suppliers. Status flow: Draft → Open → Paid (or Void).

Similar to invoices but for your expenses. When marked as Paid, the system creates a journal entry: Debit Expense, Credit Cash.

2.5 Revenue & Expenses

Record money coming in (Revenue) and money going out (Expenses).

  • Record Revenue: Select an Income account, enter the Payer name, Amount, and Payment Method.
  • Record Expense: Select an Expense account, enter the Payee name, Amount, and Payment Method.

Each entry auto-creates a journal entry and updates account balances.

2.6 Financial Reports

Located under Finance → Reports tab:

  • Profit & Loss: Revenue – Expenses = Net Profit
  • Balance Sheet: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
  • Trial Balance: All accounts with debit and credit balances

All reports use live data. Each report has a Print button.

3. HR Module

3.1 Employees

Maintain employee records including name, email, phone, department, position, employment type, hire date, and basic salary. Each employee row has View, Print, Edit, and Delete actions. Click an employee card to see full details.

3.2 Attendance

Track clock-in/clock-out times. Select an employee, set the date, and enter check-in and check-out times. You can view attendance history and print reports.

3.3 Leave Management

Employees request leave by selecting the type (Annual, Sick, Maternity, Paternity, Unpaid), start and end dates, and a reason. Managers see pending requests with Approve/Reject buttons. Approved leave is recorded in the system.

3.4 Payroll

Run payroll for a specific period. Set the period start, period end, and payment date. Each employee's basic pay, allowances, and deductions are loaded automatically. You can add or edit allowances and deductions per run. Click "Run Payroll" — the system creates journal entries automatically (Debit Salary Expense, Credit Cash).

3.5 Recruitment

Post job openings with title, department, type, location, salary range, description, duties, qualifications, requirements, and application method. You can specify how candidates should apply: via email, online portal, hand delivery, or both. Each job posting can be viewed in detail, printed as a formal job letter, shared, edited, closed, or deleted. The printed letter includes numbered duties and qualifications with the company header and footer.

4. Sales Module

4.1 Sales Orders

Sales orders track customer purchases. Status flow: Draft → Confirmed → Processing → Shipped → Delivered → Invoiced → Paid (or Cancelled).

1
Create Order: Go to Sales → Orders. Click "+ New Order". Select a customer (search existing or type a new name). Set the order date. Add line items: search/select products, enter quantity, price, discount, and tax rate. The system calculates subtotal, tax, and grand total automatically. Click "Create Order".

Each order has a dynamic status button (e.g., "Confirm", "Ship", "Deliver"). Click to advance the order through the workflow. You can also print the order.

4.2 Quotations

Quotations are price estimates for customers before they commit to a purchase. Create a quotation with customer details and items. If the customer accepts, click "Convert to Order" to turn it into a Sales Order instantly.

4.3 Pricing

Manage product selling prices. The pricing tab shows all products with their cost price, selling price, and margin percentage. Click "Edit Price" on any row to update the selling price directly.

5. Inventory Module

5.1 Stock Overview

View real-time stock levels for all products. Products below the reorder level are highlighted in red. You can filter by category and search by name or SKU. Use the "Add Product" button to create new products with name, SKU (auto-generated if blank), category, type, unit, cost price, selling price, and reorder level.

5.2 Stock Movements

Record stock in/out with different movement types: Receipt, Issue, Transfer In, Transfer Out, Adjustment, Return, Production, Consumption. Select a product, choose the movement type, optionally select a warehouse, enter quantity and unit cost. Stock levels update immediately.

5.3 Warehouses

Manage storage locations. Add warehouses with a code, name, and address. Click a warehouse card to view products stored in it, along with their quantities. You can edit or delete warehouses.

5.4 Stock Transfers

Move stock between warehouses. Select a product, the source warehouse, the destination warehouse, and the quantity. The system creates two movements: a transfer out from the source and a transfer in to the destination.

6. Supply Chain Module

6.1 Purchase Orders

Create purchase orders to order goods from suppliers. Select a supplier, set the order date and expected delivery date. Add line items by searching/selecting products and entering quantity and unit price. The system calculates the total automatically.

6.2 Goods Receiving

Receive goods against a purchase order. Select the PO, choose the receiving warehouse, and enter the quantities received for each item. The system creates inventory movements (type: receipt) and updates stock levels. If all items are fully received, the PO status changes to "delivered".

6.3 Suppliers

Manage supplier contacts. Add suppliers with company name, contact person, email, and phone. Suppliers are shared with the Contacts module. You can edit or delete suppliers from this tab.

6.4 Requisitions

Create internal purchase requests. Select a product, enter the quantity, reason, requester name, department, and priority (Low, Medium, High). Requisitions can be reviewed before being converted to purchase orders.

7. Manufacturing Module

Manufacturing is available on Pro and Enterprise plans only.

7.1 Bill of Materials (BOM)

Define the components needed to produce a finished product. Select the finished product and add components — search/select products, enter quantity and unit. A product can have only one active BOM at a time.

7.2 Work Orders

Create production orders. Select the product to produce, enter the quantity, and set scheduled start/end dates. Status flow: Draft → Confirmed → Processing → Completed (or Cancelled). Use the "Next" button to advance the work order through stages. Once completed, you can log production output or send it to Quality Control.

7.3 Shop Floor Terminal

Log production output and scrap. Select a work order (must be in Processing or Confirmed status), enter the output quantity and scrap quantity. The system creates inventory movements: production receipt for output and consumption for scrap. The work order is marked as completed.

7.4 Quality Control

Record pass/fail inspections for completed work orders. Select a work order, enter inspection notes, and choose Pass or Fail. If passed, the work order stays completed. If failed, it returns to "processing" for rework.

8. Contacts & Products

The Contacts module manages customers, suppliers, and partners. Add contacts with company name, contact person, email, phone, and address. Contacts are shared across the system — used in Sales Orders, Purchase Orders, and Invoices.

The Products module is your product catalog. Add products with name, SKU, category, type, unit, cost price, selling price, and reorder level. Products sync with Inventory for stock tracking, Sales for pricing, and Supply Chain for purchasing. The category field supports autocomplete — type a new category or select from existing ones.

9. Orders (Unified View)

The Orders module shows all order types in one place: Sales Orders, Purchase Orders, and Work Orders. Use the filter dropdown to view a specific type or see all at once. Each row shows the order number, type, customer/supplier, date, total, and status. This is useful for getting a quick overview of all pending and completed orders across your business.

10. Reports

The Reports module has two tabs:

  • Saved Reports: Create custom report templates by selecting a module (Finance, HR, Sales, Inventory, Supply Chain, Manufacturing, or All) and a type (Tabular, Chart, Summary). Click "Run" to execute the report with live data. Results display as formatted tables with summary cards. You can print any report.
  • Report Builder: Create a new report by entering a name, selecting the module and type, and adding a description. The report is saved and can be run anytime from the Saved Reports tab.

Additionally, the Finance module has its own reports tab with Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, and Trial Balance — all using real-time account data.

11. Settings

The Settings module contains 8 tabs for configuring your company's ERP experience.

11.1 Company Settings

Set your company name, legal name, tax ID, registration number, currency (KSh, USD, EUR, GBP), contact email, phone, website, and physical address. These details appear on printed documents and reports.

11.2 User Management

Invite staff members to your company. Enter their first name, last name, email, password, and role (Company Admin, Accountant, HR Manager, Sales Manager, Inventory Manager, or Staff). You can view all users, edit their details, or delete them. Each role has specific permissions that control which modules they can access.

11.3 Module Toggles

Enable or disable modules for your company. Toggle switches let you turn modules on/off instantly. Modules not included in your plan appear grayed out with an "Upgrade plan" label. Changes take effect immediately — disabled modules are hidden from the sidebar and API access is blocked.

11.4 AI Settings

Configure how AI works for your company. Choose between "Use HDM AI" (default) or "Bring Your Own Key". If using your own key, select the provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepSeek, Google Gemini, Mistral, Cohere), enter the model name and API key. You can also set module scopes — select which modules the AI can access for analysis.

11.5 API Keys

Generate outward API keys for external applications to access your ERP data. Each key has a name and module scopes. When you generate a key, copy it immediately — it won't be shown again. You can revoke keys at any time. The page also displays the API base URL and an example curl command for integration.

11.6 Billing & Plans

View your current plan, status, and subscription expiry date. See all available plans with pricing in your company's currency. To upgrade, contact support. (Upgrade functionality is restricted for security.)

11.7 Security

Change your password by entering your current password and a new one. View and manage trusted devices — remove any device you no longer use. Trusted devices allow login without re-activation.

11.8 Backups

Configure automatic backups with frequency (Daily, Weekly, Monthly), time, and retention settings (keep days, max backups). Click "Backup Now" to run an immediate backup. The backup history shows all past backups with download, share, and delete options.

12. AI Assistant

HDM ERP includes a built-in AI assistant. Click the sparkle button (bottom-right corner) to open the chat window. The AI can answer questions about your business data in real time:

  • "What is my revenue this month?"
  • "Show low stock products"
  • "List all unpaid invoices"
  • "How is my business performing?"
  • "Give me a business health overview"

The AI reads only the data you've allowed through module scopes. It displays monetary values in your company's set currency. You can clear the chat history anytime. If the AI is disabled by your administrator or not included in your plan, the sparkle button will not appear.

Tip: Be specific with your questions for the most accurate analysis. The AI analyzes real-time data from your ERP modules.

13. Desktop & Mobile Apps

Desktop App: Available for Windows, Mac, and Linux. The desktop app provides a native experience with a custom title bar (minimize, maximize, close), system tray icon, and auto-updater. It loads the full ERP interface and connects to the live server for data. Download from the landing page when enabled by the administrator.

Mobile App: Available on Android and iOS. Features include a barcode scanner for quick product lookup, offline mode for areas with poor connectivity, biometric login (fingerprint/face), and push notifications for important alerts. Download from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

Both apps use the same credentials as the web version. Your data syncs in real time across all devices.

14. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I reset my password?
A: Contact your company admin. They can reset your password from Settings → Users.

Q: Can I upgrade my plan later?
A: Yes. Contact support to upgrade from any plan to a higher tier.

Q: How secure is my data?
A: All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We use JWT authentication, role-based access control, and strict tenant isolation — your data is never accessible to other companies.

Q: Can I export my data?
A: Yes. All records can be exported to CSV or PDF. Backups can be scheduled automatically from Settings → Backups.

Q: What currencies do you support?
A: KSh (Kenyan Shilling), USD, EUR, and GBP. Your company admin can change the currency anytime from Settings → Company.

Q: How does the AI assistant work?
A: The AI analyzes your actual business data (invoices, products, employees, etc.) and answers questions in plain language. It respects module scopes set in AI Settings.

Q: Can I use my own AI provider?
A: Yes! In Settings → AI, switch to "Bring Your Own Key" and enter your API key from OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepSeek, Gemini, Mistral, or Cohere.

Q: What is an outward API key?
A: It allows external applications to access your ERP data securely. Generate keys from Settings → API Keys with specific module scopes.

15. Troubleshooting

Issue: "Device not trusted" error
You need to activate this device. Go to the activation page and enter your license key. If you lost your key, contact support.

Issue: Module not appearing in sidebar
The module may be disabled in your plan or toggled off in Settings → Modules. Check with your company admin.

Issue: AI sparkle button not showing
The AI may be disabled by the super admin or not included in your plan. Check Settings → AI.

Issue: Page not loading or blank screen
Clear your browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Del), ensure cookies are enabled, and try a different browser. Check your internet connection.

Issue: Invoice/bill not updating account balances
Ensure you have accounts set up in Finance → Accounts. The system needs at least one Asset account and one Income/Expense account to post journal entries automatically.

Issue: Login keeps redirecting
Clear localStorage and sessionStorage in your browser, then try again. If using "Remember me", your token may have expired.

Issue: Report shows no data
Ensure you have records in the relevant module. Run a report only after adding data (invoices, products, employees, etc.).
